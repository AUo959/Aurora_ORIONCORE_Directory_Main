Layer: L2  
Status: STAGING

## Scope

This document is the cleaned L2 entry point for the recovered Galactic Union material.
It reconciles the recovered text against the current GUMAS engine where corroboration exists,
while preserving unresolved extensions as draft-only.

## Recovered Scenario Spine

The clearest narrative spine in the recovery is a Zylox-era security settlement built around
an anti-coup calculus:

- Zylox needs military support but fears an internal coup.
- Core worlds receive expanded Marshal authority and combat modernization, including shielded
  power-suit development.
- Outer colonies are pushed toward semi-autonomous sheriff garrisons funded through public-
  private trade arrangements.
- The trade coalition funds defense in exchange for free-trade privileges.
- The standing military is redeployed toward frontline conflicts while new mixed federal /
  private structures maintain internal order.
- Ambitious military leaders are to be managed through prestigious but dangerous appointments.

This is a strong policy-seed for the Galactic Union and appears consistent with the later
engine emphasis on bias, legitimacy, trust, and conflict escalation.

## Core L2 World Model Recovered

### Galactic Union

- governance: senate plus chancellor executive
- doctrine: strategic adaptability
- specialist forces: Sentinels and Marshals
- diplomacy: trust-based reputation tracking
- economy: resource and supply-chain centered
- strategic pressure: three-way competition among Union, AI, and corporate actors

### Recovered Faction Set

The recovery clearly identifies these major blocs:

- Galactic Union
- AI Warlords
- Separatist Movements
- Corporate PMCs
- Velar Imperium
- Crimson Pact

Later engine files expand and rename parts of this set, but the underlying structure is stable.

## Recovered Polity Notes

### Prime Construct

The recovery already frames Prime Construct as a cooperative sovereign AI rather than a generic
enemy. That distinction appears to survive into the engine and should be treated as a core
L2 truth candidate.

### Velar Imperium

The registry fragment describes the Imperium as culturally significant but fractured, with
internal loyalist, reformist, and warlord tendencies. This aligns well with later multi-faction
pressure modeling and should remain in the L2 staging set.

### Crimson Pact

Recovered as an emerging cultic or extremist force. Current engine materials also retain
Crimson Pact as a meaningful faction slot, so this recovery likely captures an early seed form.

## Recommended L2 Staging Verdict

### Promote toward structured staging

The following should be treated as high-confidence staging material:

- Galactic Union governance model
- Zylox-era anti-coup security doctrine
- Prime Construct diplomatic split
- Vael Saros, Durn, Norr, Valcor, Arcturus, Deyrus, and Anaya as major Union figures
- Velar Imperium and Crimson Pact as stable faction anchors

### Hold as draft-only extensions

The following should remain separate from current canon until matched to more sources:

- the `Judicator Prime` operational cast and ship cluster
- any claim that a given mechanic or optimization was already permanently "updated"
- detailed policy outcomes not corroborated in engine state

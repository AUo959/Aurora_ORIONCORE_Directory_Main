## E1. Marshals governance & oversight (thread: MAS - Glactic Union)

> **Who Controls the Galactic Marshals?**
> - **The Galactic Senate** — sets their budget; passes laws that expand or restrict their authority.
> - **The Chief Marshal (Head of the Marshals)** — highest-ranking Marshal, **confirmed by the Senate**; reports to the **Judicial Council, not the Chancellor**; sets day-to-day priorities, hiring levels, directs operations.
> - **The High Chancellor (Zylox)** — influences funding via Senate budget process; **appoints the Chief Marshal (with Senate approval)**; can propose laws; **cannot give orders to the Marshals in criminal investigations**.

> The Galactic Marshals are an **independent law enforcement agency** … Their **chain of command is separate from the military and planetary police forces**, ensuring they are not used as a political tool. However, they are still subject to government oversight.

## E2. Jurisdiction & mandate

> **Jurisdiction:** Crimes that cross planetary or sector boundaries. Handle: organized crime (smugglers, syndicates, corporate corruption); political crimes (assassinations, bribery, election tampering); fugitives escaping planetary justice systems. Often work with local planetary police forces but **have authority to override them**. Operate with **diplomatic immunity** to chase criminals across borders.
> **Weaknesses:** Ineffective in Outer Colonies due to lack of presence; sometimes undermined by corrupt local governments.

Peer agencies in the same security ecosystem (context): Galactic Security Bureau (GSB), Cyber-Security Division (CSD), Union Defense Fleet, Sector Defense Forces (militias), Outer Colony Sheriff Garrison System, Trade Security Forces (TSF), Special Operations Command (SOCOM).
